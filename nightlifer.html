Nightlifer

Responsive Web App Role UI Design UX Research UX Writing

NightLifer is a location-based recommendation app that helps users navigate through the nightlife scene, find information about bars & clubs and build a trusting community by reviewing businesses.

Hypothesis

Navigating a city's nightlife can make having fun difficult. This is especially true if you're new to a city or just visiting. The NightLifer app helps users discover new places and find their way around a city's nightlife scene. It includes all the information needed to search for bars, clubs, and events. It even saves your favorite places and allows you to connect with your friends.

Objective

To design a responsive web app for location-based recommendations that meets the requirements of its users and solves the problems they face with existing location-based recommendation apps.

Problem Overview

After conducting a competitive analysis of hospitality apps, it was revealed that there’s no product on the market which solely focuses on a city’s nightlife and party scene. To find bars or clubs on any of the existing apps without having to face unnecessary results, like restaurants or 0-24 hour shops, and finding trustworthy and up-to-date information about businesses can be frustrating.

Challenge

How to create a product that only focuses on bars & clubs, provide relevant information and live updates about business, and help users create a trusting community.

Solution

The solution is a responsive webb app, that accessible across all devices. The application offers detailed business profiles, list and map search feature, live updates about how busy a place is, and a possibility to create Bar Tours with other users.

Understanding Our Audience

The primary audience for the product is people who like to go out at night and enjoy a city’s party scene. My approach to understanding the audience was to conduct user interviews and gain insights into a potential user's perspective. I conducted 3 user interviews and created 3 user personas based on my findings as well as a Job To Be Done chart.

Determining the Features

By looking at user pain ponts and needs, I was able to come up with a list of features. The list includes:

  • Search Bar
  • Map view
  • Business details: drink/food menu, live updates, reviews
  • Search filters
  • Like Button

Given my short time frame, I wasn’t able to work on all the features I intended to include, such as Creating a Bar Tour, User Profile, Add Friends, Lost & Found, Contact Business

Ideating the App

As I was working on this project alone, I had to come up with ideas by myself. First, I analyzed the market and the competitors, then made sketches and low-fidelity wireframes based on my initial ideas, which I tested on potential users.

Sitting down with users and doing a competitive analysis helped me decide on the MVP features. After integrating all the ideas into the mid-fidelity wireframes, I conducted another usability testing to see if the user needs and the design are working together.

Style Guide

While building the product’s concept, I started establishing the brand including the color palette, typography, icons, and logo.

I wanted to create a brand, which has the vibe of the 80’s, but remains modern. To achieve that, I chose a dark purple palette with neon pop-up colors in it, and the Montserrat font.

User Flow

Since the product’s main feature is to find a bar/club and land on their profile in the fastest possible way, I created one large user flow, that shows how to access a business profile in different ways.

Final Product

The final product helps users find and like places efficiently, access detailed information about a business, open the map to navigate easier, leave and read reviews. Additionally, users can create Bar Tours with other users to plan their night out easily.

Reflection

NightLifer was a project close to me, since I like to go out and had many problems finding new places or navigating in a new city. If I had additional time and capacity, I would have loved to design the remaining features and test the product more time to fully validate the product.

This app was a massive project that I would love to do again with a team in the future. I believe this product could be a viable app, but It needs further refinement and more background research.
